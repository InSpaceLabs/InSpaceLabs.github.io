High Altitude Balloon

High altitude balloons are poor man's access to the space. Low cost, near space environment, above the 99% of the atmosphere offers a unique opportunity of communication and experimental studies.

MORE

Balloon is poor manâ€™s Space. Launching satellite to do the upper atmospheric study costs in hundreds of thousands of dollars per kg of payload while balloon costs only hundreds of dollars. Also launching balloon does not require any separate ground set up as required by launching of the rockets. Balloon takes the payload to the near space altitude of over 20 km to 40 km but below the accepted altitude of space, 100 km. These altitudes make near space far more like earth orbit than the surface of the earth. Air pressure in near space reaches 99% of a vacuum or better. Air temperatures drop to a low of -60 degrees F or colder. Cosmic radiation is over 100 times greater than at sea level. Near space is located within the ozone layer and therefore is an environment of increased damaging ultraviolet radiation. Near space is reached by helium or hydrogen-filled weather balloons.
